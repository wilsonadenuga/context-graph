**Understanding "Discretion" in this context:**

Discretion means any instance where the Secretary of State has the power, authority, or latitude to make a judgment call rather than being bound by a fixed, mandatory rule. Specifically, look for provisions where the SoS:

1. **May** (but is not required to) take an action — signalled by words like "may", "at the discretion of", "as the Secretary of State sees fit", "if the Secretary of State considers it appropriate"
2. **Has the power to decide** the outcome, criteria, or conditions — e.g. "the Secretary of State shall determine", "as the Secretary of State thinks proper"
3. **Can grant or refuse** something based on their own judgment — e.g. waivers, exemptions, extensions, permissions
4. **Can set or vary** rules, thresholds, timeframes, or requirements — e.g. "such period as the Secretary of State may specify", "prescribed by the Secretary of State"
5. **Is given subjective evaluative power** — e.g. "if the Secretary of State is satisfied that", "where the Secretary of State reasonably believes", "in the opinion of the Secretary of State"

Be thorough. Do not skip provisions just because the discretionary language is subtle. Flag anything that gives the SoS room for judgment, even if it is constrained by conditions.
